GDPR Compliance Statement

Last Updated: October 26, 2023

Our Commitment to GDPR

Bridgee Solutions ('we', 'us', or 'our') is committed to ensuring the security and protection of the personal information that we process, and to provide a compliant and consistent approach to data protection under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other relevant data protection laws. We recognize the importance of the GDPR and are dedicated to safeguarding the personal data of our clients, employees, and website users.

What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that came into effect in the European Union (EU) on May 25, 2018. It replaced previous EU data protection directives and is designed to harmonize data privacy laws across Europe, to protect and empower all EU citizens' data privacy, and to reshape the way organizations across the region approach data privacy. Even though Bridgee Solutions is based in Ethiopia, we aim to adhere to GDPR principles for any personal data we may process from individuals in the EU.

How We Comply

Bridgee Solutions strives to ensure that our data processing activities are lawful, fair, and transparent. We have reviewed our data collection and storage practices to align with GDPR principles. Key aspects of our compliance approach include:

  • Detailed information on what personal data we collect, how it is used, shared, and retained is provided in our Privacy Policy.
  • We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including protecting data against unauthorized or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.
  • We provide mechanisms for data subjects to exercise their rights under GDPR.
  • We ensure that any third-party service providers who process personal data on our behalf are also compliant with GDPR or equivalent data protection standards.

Data Subject Rights under GDPR

Under GDPR, individuals (data subjects) have several rights concerning their personal data. These include:

  • Right to access: You have the right to request copies of your personal data.
  • Right to rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate or complete information you believe is incomplete.
  • Right to erasure (‘right to be forgotten’): You have the right to request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • Right to restrict processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.
  • Right to data portability: You have the right to request that we transfer the data that we have collected to another organization, or directly to you, under certain conditions.
  • Right to object: You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data, under certain conditions.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details provided in our Privacy Policy or below.

Data Processing Activities

We process personal data for specific, legitimate purposes such as providing our outsourcing services, communicating with clients and potential clients, processing job applications, and maintaining our website. We only collect personal data that is necessary for these purposes and ensure it is processed securely. For more details, please refer to our Privacy Policy.

Data Security Measures

We have implemented appropriate technical and organizational security measures designed to protect the security of any personal information we process. These measures aim to prevent accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data.

International Data Transfers

As a company based in Ethiopia, if we transfer personal data originating from the EU/EEA to Ethiopia or other countries outside the EU/EEA, we will ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect the data in accordance with GDPR requirements. This may include relying on adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or other valid transfer mechanisms.
